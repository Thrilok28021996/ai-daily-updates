## ⚖️ AI Policy & Regulation

- **White House signed Executive Order "Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security" on June 2, 2026.** The order creates a voluntary pre-release review framework: AI developers are invited to submit frontier models to federal agencies 30 days before public release to identify and remediate security vulnerabilities. The order frames itself as a partnership with industry rather than rulemaking, explicitly declining to impose binding regulations. Source: https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/06/promoting-advanced-artificial-intelligence-innovation-and-security/ and https://www.insideprivacy.com/artificial-intelligence/white-house-releases-executive-order-on-advanced-ai-innovation-and-security/

- **US Department of Commerce issued an export-control directive on June 12, 2026**, requiring Anthropic to suspend access to Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 for any foreign national. The directive cited national security concerns after the government determined it could jailbreak Fable 5 into identifying software vulnerabilities. No CVE has been assigned and no technical vulnerability has been publicly disclosed as of June 15. Source: https://www.fifthrow.com/blog/us-export-control-order-and-global-suspension-of-fable-5-mythos-5-operationalizing-compliance-as-a-live-mandate and https://www.anthropic.com/news/fable-mythos-access

- **Bipartisan "Great American AI Act" discussion draft released June 4, 2026** by Representatives Jay Obernolte (R-CA) and Lori Trahan (D-MA). The 269-page draft would create the first comprehensive federal AI governance regime in the US, establish binding development obligations on "large frontier developers" (companies with $500M+ annual revenue that have trained a frontier model), and preempt state AI laws for three years. The draft has not been formally introduced in Congress. Critics including Public Citizen argue it would strip states' authority to protect consumers. Source: https://obernolte.house.gov/media/press-releases/obernolte-trahan-release-discussion-draft-great-american-ai-act and https://rollcall.com/2026/06/04/bipartisan-ai-draft-proposes-three-year-preemption-of-state-laws/

- **Colorado AI Act effective date delayed to January 1, 2027.** Colorado Governor Polis signed SB 189 on May 14, 2026, amending the original Colorado AI Act and moving the effective date from June 30, 2026. The amendment also scales back several of the original law's requirements. The act, when it takes effect, will impose obligations on deployers and developers of high-risk AI systems to protect residents from algorithmic discrimination in employment, healthcare, housing, financial services, and other sectors. Source: https://www.hunton.com/privacy-and-cybersecurity-law-blog/colorado-ai-act-amended-and-effective-date-delayed

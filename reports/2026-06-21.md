## 📄 AI Research Papers

- **"Immersion in the GitHub Universe: Scaling Coding Agents to Mastery" (ScaleSWE)** — arXiv:2602.09892. A pipeline that mined 6 million real GitHub pull requests across 5,200 repositories to synthesize training data, then fine-tuned Qwen3-30B-A3B-Instruct into a coding agent achieving 64% on SWE-bench Verified — a 42-point absolute improvement over the base model's 22%, exceeding the previous open-source state-of-the-art (62.4%). The team also released 20,000 SWE task instances as an open dataset. Source: [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.09892) | [GitHub](https://github.com/AweAI-Team/ScaleSWE)

- **AgileThinker** — Proposes running a cheap reactive reasoning head concurrently with a deeper planner to maintain correctness under real-time latency constraints. Benchmarks show performance advantages for time-pressured agentic settings (trading, ops monitoring, game/robot loops) where single-paradigm agents degrade. Source: [Build This Now](https://www.buildthisnow.com/blog/guide/mechanics/ai-research-june-2026)

- **AXIOM: A Trust-First Neuro-Symbolic Execution Architecture for Verifiable Mathematical Reasoning** — Pairs a reasoning agent that searches for proofs with a secondary agent that formalizes results in Lean 4 for machine-checked verification. Source: [arXiv cs.AI June 2026](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/current)

- **AgentHallu** — A 693-trajectory benchmark spanning 7 agent frameworks and 5 domains, designed to test whether models can identify which specific step in a multi-step run caused a hallucination and explain why. Source: [Build This Now](https://www.buildthisnow.com/blog/guide/mechanics/ai-research-june-2026)

- **"Latent Reward Steering"** — An adaptive inference-time framework that implicitly promotes cognitive behaviors in reasoning LLMs without explicit reward signals at test time. Source: [arXiv cs.LG June 2026](https://arxiv.org/list/cs.LG/current)

## ⚖️ AI Policy & Regulation

- **US Executive Order on AI (signed June 2, 2026)**: President Trump signed "Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security," directing AI developers to voluntarily share frontier models with the federal government up to 30 days before public release (down from a reported 90-day draft). The order directs agencies to develop AI cybersecurity benchmarks and create an "AI cybersecurity clearinghouse." It explicitly prohibits creating mandatory licensing or preclearance requirements for AI model distribution. Source: [White House](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/06/promoting-advanced-artificial-intelligence-innovation-and-security/) | [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/06/02/nx-s1-5844347/ai-safety-trump-executive-order)

- **US export control directive on Anthropic models (June 12, 2026)**: The US government issued a legally binding directive requiring Anthropic to suspend access to Fable 5 and Mythos 5 for all foreign nationals — including Anthropic's own foreign national employees — marking the first time a US frontier AI model was restricted by government order. Source: [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/06/13/anthropic-disables-fable-mythos-export-controls-national-security-threat/) | [Time](https://time.com/article/2026/06/13/anthropic-fable-mythos-ban-US-security/)

- **EU AI Act Digital Omnibus agreement (reached May 7, 2026)**: Negotiators from the EU Council, Parliament, and Commission reached a provisional agreement amending the AI Act's timelines: Annex III high-risk systems (recruitment, credit scoring, law enforcement) must now comply by December 2, 2027; Annex I systems embedded in regulated products by August 2, 2028. AI-generated content watermarking obligations were postponed from August 2 to December 2, 2026. Source: [Global Policy Watch](https://www.globalpolicywatch.com/2026/06/eu-ai-act-update-timeline-relief-targeted-simplification-and-new-prohibitions-2/) | [Inside Privacy](https://www.insideprivacy.com/artificial-intelligence/eu-ai-act-update-timeline-relief-targeted-simplification-and-new-prohibitions/)

- **EU AI Act new prohibitions**: The Digital Omnibus adds two new banned practices to Article 5, effective December 2, 2026: (1) AI systems that generate or manipulate non-consensual intimate imagery of real persons; (2) AI systems that generate or manipulate child sexual abuse material (CSAM). Source: [Inside Privacy](https://www.insideprivacy.com/artificial-intelligence/eu-ai-act-update-timeline-relief-targeted-simplification-and-new-prohibitions/)

- **Colorado SB 24-205 repealed**: Colorado's comprehensive AI governance law was repealed in May 2026 before taking effect, replaced by the narrower SB 26-189 regulating automated decision-making technology, effective January 1, 2027. Source: [Verifywise](https://verifywise.ai/blog/state-of-ai-governance-regulations-united-states-2026)

## ⚖️ AI Policy & Regulation

- On June 2, 2026, President Trump signed Executive Order "Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security." Key provisions: (1) AI developers may voluntarily submit frontier models for up to 30 days of pre-release government review; (2) federal agencies are directed to develop AI cyber-capability benchmarks and create an AI cybersecurity clearinghouse; (3) the order expressly states it does not authorize mandatory government licensing, pre-clearance, or permitting requirements for AI model development or distribution. An earlier draft had proposed a 90-day review window, which was reduced to 30 days in the final order. Source: https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/06/promoting-advanced-artificial-intelligence-innovation-and-security/

- Colorado Governor Polis signed SB 189 on May 14, 2026, repealing the original Colorado AI Act and replacing it with the Colorado Automated Decision-Making Technology Act (CADMA), effective January 1, 2027. CADMA removes the original law's algorithmic discrimination obligations, risk-management requirements, impact assessments, and broad disclosure mandates, adopting a significantly narrower approach. Source: https://www.bytebacklaw.com/2026/05/colorado-repeals-replaces-its-ai-act-what-the-new-law-means/

Source: https://www.whitehouse.gov/releases/2026/03/president-donald-j-trump-unveils-national-ai-legislative-framework/

## ⚖️ AI Policy & Regulation

- **U.S. Executive Order on AI (June 2, 2026):** President Trump signed "Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security." Key provisions: (1) AI developers are asked to voluntarily submit frontier models for government testing up to 30 days before broader release; (2) federal agencies are directed to create an AI cybersecurity clearinghouse and develop model capability benchmarks; (3) the order expressly prohibits construing it as authorizing mandatory licensing or pre-clearance requirements. Source: https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/06/promoting-advanced-artificial-intelligence-innovation-and-security/

- **EU AI Act — high-risk deadline deferred:** In May 2026, the EU Council and Parliament agreed to push back enforcement of high-risk AI system obligations by 16 months, to December 2, 2027. The amendment also removed duplicate compliance burdens for manufacturers and extended regulatory relief to smaller businesses. Source: https://www.consilium.europa.eu/en/press/press-releases/2026/05/07/artificial-intelligence-council-and-parliament-agree-to-simplify-and-streamline-rules/

- **EU AI Act — Article 50 and GPAI supervision take effect August 2, 2026:** Despite the high-risk deferral, Article 50 transparency obligations (AI chatbot disclosure, deepfake watermarking, biometric categorization disclosure) and the Commission's supervision and enforcement powers over GPAI model providers go into force on the original date. Penalties up to €35M or 7% of global revenue apply. Source: https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai

- **California AI Transparency Act (SB 942):** Takes effect August 2, 2026, requiring generative AI providers to offer watermarks, latent disclosures, and detection tools for AI-generated content. Source: https://verifywise.ai/blog/state-of-ai-governance-regulations-united-states-2026

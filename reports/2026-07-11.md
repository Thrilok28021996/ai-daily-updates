## ⚖️ AI Policy & Regulation

- **Illinois Governor JB Pritzker signed SB 315, the Artificial Intelligence Safety Measures Act, on July 6, 2026.** The law applies to AI systems generating more than $500 million in annual revenue and trained using large-scale compute. Key requirements include public disclosure of safety practices, reporting of significant safety incidents, and — for the first time in any U.S. state law — mandatory annual independent third-party audits. The law also establishes whistleblower protections for employees raising AI safety concerns. It takes effect January 1, 2027. Source: https://capitolnewsillinois.com/news/pritzker-signs-landmark-ai-regulation-bill-that-aims-to-mitigate-risks/ / https://news.wttw.com/2026/07/06/pritzker-signs-landmark-ai-regulation-bill-aims-mitigate-risks

- **The FTC published a proposed policy statement on July 1, 2026** in the Federal Register (Matter No. P264200) titled "Policy Statement Concerning the Suppression of Accuracy in Artificial Intelligence Systems." The FTC takes the position that steering an AI model's output away from a correct answer without disclosure can constitute deception under Section 5 of the FTC Act. The statement identifies modifying outputs to advance ideological goals or in response to political or public pressure as potential violations. The public comment period runs through July 31, 2026. Commission vote was 2-0. Source: https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2026/07/ftc-seeks-public-comment-policy-statement-addressing-ai-accuracy / https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/07/07/2026-13628/policy-statement-concerning-the-suppression-accuracy-in-artificial-intelligence-systems

- **U.S. states have enacted 109 AI laws and 28 data center laws as of July 1, 2026**, per TechPolicy.Press. Emerging legislative themes include child safety, data centers, and consumer protection. California, New York, and Illinois collectively represent roughly 40% of the U.S. AI market. Source: https://www.techpolicy.press/where-state-ai-legislation-stands-half-way-into-2026/

- **The EU's July 2026 cybersecurity and AI action plan** sets out a coordinated approach to help EU Member States address cybersecurity risks posed by advanced AI models, part of ongoing EU AI Act implementation. Source: https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/regulatory-framework-ai

## ⚖️ AI Policy & Regulation

- On June 4, 2026, Representatives Jay Obernolte (R-CA) and Lori Trahan (D-MA) released a discussion draft of the Great American Artificial Intelligence Act (GAAIA), a bipartisan proposal for a comprehensive federal AI governance framework. The bill primarily targets "large frontier developers" — entities with annual revenues exceeding $500 million that have trained models using more than 10²⁶ FLOPs. Key requirements include mandatory third-party audits through Independent Verification Organizations (IVOs), public disclosure of model information, and whistleblower protections. The draft would preempt state AI laws for three years. Civil penalties reach up to $1 million per violation per day. The sponsors invited public comment at GAAIA@mail.house.gov; the bill has not yet been formally introduced. Source: https://obernolte.house.gov/media/press-releases/obernolte-trahan-release-discussion-draft-great-american-ai-act

- Colorado's AI Act (SB 24-205) takes full effect on June 30, 2026, becoming the most detailed AI-specific consumer protection law in the United States. The law requires deployers of high-risk AI systems (those making consequential decisions in employment, education, financial services, healthcare, housing, insurance, or legal services) to complete impact assessments, annual reviews, and provide transparency and correction mechanisms to consumers. The Colorado Attorney General holds exclusive enforcement authority, with penalties up to $20,000 per violation. The 60-day statutory cure period that applied during the transitional year expires with the law taking full effect. Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colorado_AI_Act

- The White House issued a presidential action titled "Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security" on June 4, 2026, extending the administration's federal AI framework and addressing national security dimensions of AI development. Source: https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/06/promoting-advanced-artificial-intelligence-innovation-and-security/

- Texas's Responsible Artificial Intelligence Governance Act (TRAIGA), enacted in June 2025, has been in effect since January 1, 2026, establishing a state-level AI governance regime that applies to high-risk AI deployments in Texas. Source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TRAIGA

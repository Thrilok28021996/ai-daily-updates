## 📄 AI Research Papers

- "Self-Compacting Language Model Agents" (arXiv:2606.23525), submitted June 22, 2026, proposes SelfCompact — a scaffold that lets the model itself decide when and how to compress accumulated context during long agentic tasks. Authors include researchers from Johns Hopkins and Google DeepMind. The paper shows that a compaction tool alone is used inconsistently by open-weight models and requires a lightweight rubric specifying trigger conditions (sub-task resolved, trajectory converging) and suppression conditions (mid-derivation, stuck state). Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.23525

- "Benchmarking Agentic Review Systems" (arXiv:2606.19749) appeared on arXiv in late June 2026, evaluating automated systems designed to perform peer review at scale, a topic that gained practical relevance after AAAI-26 ran an AI-assisted review pilot. Source: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2606.19749

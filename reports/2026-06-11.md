## ⚖️ AI Policy & Regulation

- **The "Great American AI Act" discussion draft was released on June 4, 2026** by Representatives Jay Obernolte (R-CA) and Lori Trahan (D-MA). The 269-page bipartisan bill is the first proposed comprehensive federal AI governance framework in the United States. Key provisions include: mandatory third-party audits of frontier models via Independent Verification Organizations (IVOs); whistleblower protections; disclosure requirements for frontier developers with over $500M annual revenue; and a three-year preemption of state laws on AI development (but not AI deployment). Source: https://obernolte.house.gov/media/press-releases/obernolte-trahan-release-discussion-draft-great-american-ai-act

- **The Great American AI Act defines "catastrophic risk"** as a foreseeable risk of death or serious injury to more than 50 people, or more than $1 billion in property damage, caused by a model assisting with weapons of mass destruction, cyberattacks, or harmful autonomous action without meaningful human oversight. Source: https://fedscoop.com/bipartisan-great-american-ai-act-draft-proposes-new-federal-ai-governance-framework/

- **The bill includes AI-related WARN Act amendments**: if AI was a "substantial factor" in a qualifying mass layoff, employers must now disclose the type of AI used, estimate the percentage of losses attributable to AI, and describe any retraining steps taken.

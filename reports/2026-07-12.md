## 📄 AI Research Papers

- **"Single-Rollout Asynchronous Optimization (SAO) for Agentic Reinforcement Learning"** (arXiv:2607.07508, July 2026): Proposes replacing group-wise rollout sampling with single-rollout sampling per prompt to stabilize asynchronous RL training for LLMs. Introduces a strict double-sided token-level clipping strategy. SAO trained stably for 1,000 steps and outperformed GRPO and its variants on agentic coding and reasoning benchmarks. Applied in the training pipeline for GLM-5.2. Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07508

- **"EmbodiedGen: Towards a Generative 3D World Engine for Embodied Intelligence"** (arXiv:2506.10600, Horizon Robotics and WuwenAI): Presents a 3D world engine that generates executable, physically plausible, and interactive simulation environments for embodied AI. Policies trained in generated environments achieved an 88.9% real-world success rate for cube stacking and improved sim-to-real transfer from 21.7% to 75.0% for mobile manipulation. Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.10600

- **"From Signals to Structure: How Memory Architecture Drives Language Emergence in LLM Agents"** (arXiv, July 2026): Covers how different memory architectures influence the emergence of language and communication conventions in multi-agent LLM systems. Source: https://arxiv.org/list/cs.MA/current

## ⚖️ AI Policy & Regulation

- Illinois Governor JB Pritzker signed SB 315, the Artificial Intelligence Safety Measures Act, into law on July 6, 2026. The law applies to AI developers with systems generating over $500 million in annual revenue trained with large-scale compute. It mandates: (1) public disclosure of safety practices, (2) reporting of significant safety incidents, (3) annual independent third-party safety audits — the first such requirement in any U.S. state law — and (4) plans to mitigate "catastrophic risk" including harms that could "materially contribute to the death of, or serious injury to, more than 50 people." Civil penalties up to $1 million for a first offense and $3 million for subsequent violations. Source: https://capitolnewsillinois.com/news/pritzker-signs-landmark-ai-regulation-bill-that-aims-to-mitigate-risks/

- The United Nations held the inaugural Global Dialogue on AI Governance in Geneva on July 6–7, 2026, convening all 193 UN Member States alongside industry, civil society, and academia. UN Secretary-General António Guterres stated that "science currently cannot guarantee that increasingly capable AI systems will not cause catastrophic harm" and called for international agreements, including an AI Child Safety Pledge. A second session is scheduled in New York in May 2027. Source: https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/07/1167862

- The U.S. Federal Trade Commission published a proposed policy statement on July 1, 2026, titled "Suppression of Accuracy in Artificial Intelligence Systems," open for public comment until July 31, 2026. The statement holds that steering an AI model's outputs away from accurate responses without disclosure can constitute deception under Section 5 of the FTC Act. The statement also asserts that Colorado's AI Act, which pressures AI companies to avoid disparate impact, may be impliedly preempted by federal law to the extent it conflicts with Section 5. Source: https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2026/07/ftc-seeks-public-comment-policy-statement-addressing-ai-accuracy

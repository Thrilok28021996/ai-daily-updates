## ⚖️ AI Policy & Regulation

- President Trump signed an executive order titled "Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security" on June 2, 2026. The order establishes a voluntary framework under which developers of advanced AI models may provide the federal government with pre-release model access for cybersecurity and national security assessments. Within 60 days, the Treasury, NSA, and CISA must develop a classified benchmarking process for advanced cyber capabilities. The order expressly prohibits mandatory governmental licensing or permitting requirements for AI model development or release. Source: https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/06/promoting-advanced-artificial-intelligence-innovation-and-security/

- The European Parliament approved amendments to the EU AI Act on June 16, 2026 by a vote of 423 to 57 (174 abstentions). Key changes include: delaying the compliance deadline for high-risk AI systems (e.g., hiring, education, law enforcement) from August 2, 2026 to December 2, 2027; pushing AI embedded in regulated products (medical devices, machinery) to August 2, 2028; introducing an outright ban on AI tools used to generate non-consensual intimate imagery; and simplifying overlap rules for AI in industrial machinery. EU Council sign-off is pending and expected before August 2, 2026. Source: https://www.morganlewis.com/pubs/2026/06/eu-approves-delays-and-other-amendments-to-certain-eu-ai-act-obligations-what-businesses-should-know

## ⚖️ AI Policy & Regulation

- Illinois Governor JB Pritzker signed the Artificial Intelligence Safety Measures Act (SB 315) into law on July 6, 2026. The law applies to frontier AI developers generating more than $500 million in annual revenue. Key requirements include: mandatory annual independent third-party safety audits (a first among US state laws), publication of a frontier AI risk framework covering catastrophic-risk assessment, cybersecurity, and internal governance, and reporting of critical safety incidents to the state within 72 hours (or within 24 hours if the incident poses imminent risk of death or serious injury). Civil penalties are up to $1 million for a first offense and up to $3 million for subsequent violations. Source: https://capitolnewsillinois.com/news/pritzker-signs-landmark-ai-regulation-bill-that-aims-to-mitigate-risks/

- The Illinois, California, and New York AI laws collectively cover an estimated 40% of the US AI market, creating what lawmakers describe as a de facto national standard in the absence of federal legislation. As of July 1, US states have enacted a cumulative total of 109 AI laws and 28 data center laws. Source: https://www.techpolicy.press/where-state-ai-legislation-stands-half-way-into-2026/

- The FTC released a proposed policy statement on July 1, 2026 addressing "suppression of accuracy" in AI systems, open for public comment through July 31. The statement argues that steering an AI model's output away from factually correct answers without user disclosure constitutes deception under Section 5 of the FTC Act. Companies may prioritize non-accuracy objectives but must disclose this clearly and conspicuously to users. Source: https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2026/07/ftc-seeks-public-comment-policy-statement-addressing-ai-accuracy

- The UN Global Dialogue on AI Governance convened in Geneva on July 6–7, 2026, bringing together international stakeholders to discuss coordinated approaches to AI oversight, including a coordinated cybersecurity and AI action plan to address resilience challenges posed by advanced AI models. Source: https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/07/1167862

- The White House released its National Policy Framework for Artificial Intelligence on March 20, 2026, containing legislative recommendations for a unified national approach to AI governance and establishing a voluntary framework for frontier model developers to provide government preview access before public release. Source: https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2026/03/03.20.26-National-Policy-Framework-for-Artificial-Intelligence-Legislative-Recommendations.pdf

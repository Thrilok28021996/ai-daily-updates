## ⚖️ AI Policy & Regulation

- **Illinois Governor Pritzker signed the Artificial Intelligence Safety Measures Act (SB 315) on July 6, 2026.** The law requires large frontier AI developers to publish safety frameworks, conduct mandatory annual third-party audits (a first-in-the-nation requirement), report incidents to the state within 72 hours (24 hours if there is imminent risk of death or serious physical injury), and provide whistleblower protections for employees raising AI safety concerns. Source: https://capitolnewsillinois.com/news/pritzker-signs-landmark-ai-regulation-bill-that-aims-to-mitigate-risks/

- **The FTC published a proposed policy statement on July 1, 2026 addressing "suppression of accuracy in artificial intelligence systems."** The statement argues that concealing how AI outputs are steered away from accuracy or objectivity may constitute an unfair or deceptive practice under Section 5 of the FTC Act. The Commission vote to publish for comment was 2-0. Public comments are open through July 31, 2026. Source: https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2026/07/ftc-seeks-public-comment-policy-statement-addressing-ai-accuracy

- **The White House's March 2026 National Policy Framework for Artificial Intelligence recommends preempting state AI regulation of developers**, including provisions that would bar states from penalizing AI developers for third-party unlawful conduct involving their models. The framework remains a legislative recommendation, not enacted law. Source: https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/06/promoting-advanced-artificial-intelligence-innovation-and-security/

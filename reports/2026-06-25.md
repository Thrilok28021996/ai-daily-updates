## ⚖️ AI Policy & Regulation

- **European Parliament voted 423–57 on June 16, 2026 to amend the EU AI Act for the first time.** Key changes: high-risk AI obligations (Annex III stand-alone systems) pushed to December 2, 2027; Annex I embedded systems to August 2, 2028. A ban on AI-generated non-consensual intimate imagery ("nudifier" apps) was added, with compliance required by December 2, 2026. AI-generated content watermarking/labelling (Article 50(2)) extended to December 2, 2026. The Council must formally adopt the text before it enters into force, expected before August 2, 2026. Sources: https://natlawreview.com/article/eu-ai-act-amended-parliament-votes-delay-key-deadlines; https://www.matheson.com/insights/eu-parliament-approves-amendments-to-the-ai-act/; https://sofiaglobe.com/2026/06/16/european-parliament-approves-ai-act-amendments-nudifier-ban/

- **President Trump signed the executive order "Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security" on June 2, 2026.** The order explicitly bars mandatory government licensing or preclearance for AI model releases. It establishes a voluntary framework under which AI developers may submit frontier models for government evaluation up to 30 days before public release. It also directs agencies to create an AI cybersecurity clearinghouse for sharing vulnerability information and to develop benchmarks for assessing AI models' cyber capabilities. Sources: https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/06/promoting-advanced-artificial-intelligence-innovation-and-security/; https://www.npr.org/2026/06/02/nx-s1-5844347/ai-safety-trump-executive-order; https://www.freshfields.com/en/our-thinking/blogs/a-fresh-take/trump-executive-order-on-ai-voluntary-framework-cybersecurity-focus-and-key-ta-102n18b

- **Colorado enacted SB 26-189 in May 2026**, repealing and replacing the original Colorado AI Act with a narrower law regulating "automated decision-making technology" (ADMT) used in consequential decisions (education, employment, housing, financial services, insurance, healthcare, government services). Requires deployers to provide notice, an adverse action process, and record retention for three years. Effective January 1, 2027; implementing regulations due by the same date.

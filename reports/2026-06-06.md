## ⚖️ AI Policy & Regulation

- **Trump Administration signed an executive order** on June 2, 2026 titled "Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security." Key provisions: (1) directs federal agencies to create a voluntary framework within 60 days under which developers may submit frontier AI models for government evaluation up to 30 days before public release; (2) directs creation of an "AI cybersecurity clearinghouse" to review and share vulnerability information; (3) expressly prohibits creation of any mandatory governmental licensing, pre-clearance, or permitting requirement for AI model development or release. Source: https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/06/promoting-advanced-artificial-intelligence-innovation-and-security/ ; https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/02/trump-executive-order-ai.html ; https://www.npr.org/2026/06/02/nx-s1-5844347/ai-safety-trump-executive-order

- **Colorado rewrote its AI law** (SB 26-189), signed into law ahead of the previously scheduled June 30, 2026 enforcement date. The amended law: removes the duty of care to prevent algorithmic discrimination, eliminates deployer requirements for risk management programs and impact assessments, narrows scope to disclosure and transparency requirements around automated decision-making technologies (ADMT), delays enforcement to January 1, 2027, and is enforceable only by the Colorado attorney general (no private right of action). Violations carry potential civil penalties of up to $20,000 per violation. Source: https://www.nortonrosefulbright.com/en-us/knowledge/publications/18733d31/colorado-enacts-revised-ai-law ; https://www.hunton.com/privacy-and-cybersecurity-law-blog/colorado-ai-act-amended-and-effective-date-delayed ; https://www.consumerfinancemonitor.com/2026/05/12/colorado-rewrites-its-landmark-ai-law-unpacking-sb-26-189-and-what-it-means-for-businesses/

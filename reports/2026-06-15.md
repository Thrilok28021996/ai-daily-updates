## ⚖️ AI Policy & Regulation

- **Colorado** Governor Jared Polis signed SB 26-189 on May 14, 2026, repealing and replacing the original Colorado Artificial Intelligence Act (SB 24-205). The new law shifts from a risk-management and duty-of-care framework to a transparency-based disclosure model. It takes effect January 1, 2027. Source: https://www.hklaw.com/en/insights/publications/2026/05/colorado-governor-signs-sb-189

- A federal magistrate judge issued a stay on enforcement of the original Colorado AI Act on April 27, 2026. The U.S. Department of Justice also joined a lawsuit by xAI challenging the law's constitutionality. Source: https://www.lawandtheworkplace.com/2026/05/major-developments-put-colorados-ai-law-on-ice-ahead-of-implementation/

- The **White House** released a National Policy Framework for Artificial Intelligence on March 20, 2026, urging Congress to establish a federal AI framework that would preempt state laws imposing what the administration characterizes as "undue burdens." The framework does not create immediate compliance obligations; legislative action by Congress is required. Source: https://www.wilmerhale.com/en/insights/blogs/wilmerhale-privacy-and-cybersecurity-law/20260323-white-house-releases-national-policy-framework-for-artificial-intelligence

- **Japan's** Act on the Promotion of Research and Development and Utilisation of AI-Related Technologies entered into effect in June 2026.

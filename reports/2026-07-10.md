## ⚖️ AI Policy & Regulation

- Illinois Governor JB Pritzker signed SB 315, the Artificial Intelligence Safety Measures Act, on July 6, 2026. The law requires AI developers generating more than $500 million in annual revenue from large-scale models to publish annual risk-mitigation plans and submit to mandatory annual third-party safety audits — the first such third-party audit requirement in any U.S. state law. Civil penalties are $1 million for a first violation and $3 million for subsequent violations. The law takes effect January 1, 2028. Source: https://capitolnewsillinois.com/news/pritzker-signs-landmark-ai-regulation-bill-that-aims-to-mitigate-risks/

- The FTC published a proposed policy statement on July 1, 2026 — "Suppression of Accuracy in Artificial Intelligence Systems" (Federal Register: 2026-13628) — and is accepting public comment through July 31, 2026. The statement asserts that AI companies that secretly tune outputs to pursue undisclosed ideological objectives, rather than user intent, may violate the "unfair or deceptive acts" prohibition in Section 5 of the FTC Act. The FTC cites data suggesting consumers accept AI outputs without independent fact-checking more than 90% of the time. Source: https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2026/07/ftc-seeks-public-comment-policy-statement-addressing-ai-accuracy

- The Trump administration signed Executive Order 14409, "Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security," on June 2, 2026. It establishes a voluntary framework allowing frontier AI developers to share model access with the federal government for up to 30 days before public release. It also directs relevant agencies to build a classified benchmarking process for assessing the cyber capabilities of frontier models and to stand up an AI Cybersecurity Clearinghouse by July 2, 2026. Source: https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/06/promoting-advanced-artificial-intelligence-innovation-and-security/

- As of July 1, 2026, U.S. states have enacted a cumulative total of 109 AI-related laws and 28 data center laws, according to tracking by TechPolicy.Press. Emerging legislative themes include child safety, data center siting, and consumer protection. Source: https://www.techpolicy.press/where-state-ai-legislation-stands-half-way-into-2026/

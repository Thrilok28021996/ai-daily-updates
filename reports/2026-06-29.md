## ⚖️ AI Policy & Regulation

- On June 2, 2026, President Trump signed an executive order titled "Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security," establishing a voluntary (non-mandatory) framework under which AI developers may share new models with federal national security agencies up to 30 days before public or partner release for cybersecurity assessment. The order explicitly bars mandatory licensing, preclearance, or permitting requirements for AI development or distribution. Source: https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2026/06/promoting-advanced-artificial-intelligence-innovation-and-security/ / https://www.hklaw.com/en/insights/publications/2026/06/executive-order-on-artificial-intelligence-expands-cybersecurity

- The European Parliament on June 16, 2026 granted final approval to amendments to the EU AI Act. Key changes: (1) Annex III high-risk AI systems (education, employment, credit scoring, law enforcement, etc.) compliance deadline extended from August 2, 2026 to December 2, 2027 (a 16-month delay); (2) product safety law high-risk systems extended from August 2, 2027 to August 2, 2028 (12-month delay); (3) "nudifier" applications (non-consensual intimate imagery generators) newly classified as prohibited systems effective December 2, 2026; (4) watermarking obligations for pre-August 2026 systems also delayed to December 2, 2026. The European Council is expected to formally adopt the amendments by August 2, 2026. Source: https://www.morganlewis.com/pubs/2026/06/eu-approves-delays-and-other-amendments-to-certain-eu-ai-act-obligations-what-businesses-should-know / https://www.globalpolicywatch.com/2026/06/eu-ai-act-update-timeline-relief-targeted-simplification-and-new-prohibitions-2/

- California's AI Transparency Act (SB 942) takes effect August 2, 2026 (date pushed back from January 1, 2026 by AB 853). It applies to generative AI providers with over 1 million monthly users accessible in California, requiring: a free public AI-content detection tool, an option for users to add visible AI disclosures to generated content, and machine-readable provenance watermarks in AI-generated images, video, and audio. The California Attorney General can bring civil actions with fines up to $5,000 per daily violation. Source: https://www.jonesday.com/en/insights/2024/10/california-enacts-ai-transparency-law-requiring-disclosures-for-ai-content / https://www.ailawsbystate.com/blog/california-ai-transparency-act-sb-942

Source: https://verifywise.ai/blog/state-of-ai-governance-regulations-united-states-2026

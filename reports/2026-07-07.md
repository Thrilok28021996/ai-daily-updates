## ⚖️ AI Policy & Regulation

- Illinois Governor JB Pritzker signed SB 315, the Artificial Intelligence Safety Measures Act, into law on July 6, 2026. The law applies to "large frontier developers" generating at least $500 million in annual revenue and requires them to publish safety plans addressing catastrophic risk, report critical safety incidents within 72 hours (24 hours if posing imminent risk of death or serious injury), and undergo mandatory annual third-party audits — the first such requirement in any U.S. state law. Civil penalties reach $1 million for a first violation and $3 million for subsequent violations. The law takes effect January 1, 2028. Source: https://news.wttw.com/2026/07/06/pritzker-signs-landmark-ai-regulation-bill-aims-mitigate-risks and https://capitolnewsillinois.com/news/pritzker-signs-landmark-ai-regulation-bill-that-aims-to-mitigate-risks/

- Illinois' law also creates confidential reporting channels and whistleblower protections for employees raising AI safety concerns. Source: https://gov-pritzker-newsroom.prezly.com/gov-pritzker-signs-nation-leading-artificial-intelligence-safety-law

- The European Commission presented its EU Action Plan on Cybersecurity and Artificial Intelligence on July 7, 2026. The plan does not introduce new legislation, instead focusing on implementing the existing AI Act. Key measures include establishing an EU AI model evaluation capacity, creating a secure AI cybersecurity testing platform (via ENISA and the Joint Research Centre), launching an EU Grand Challenge on AI for cybersecurity, and defining a European blueprint for structured enterprise access to advanced AI models. Source: https://commission.europa.eu/news-and-media/news/new-eu-plan-address-risks-and-opportunities-advanced-ai-cybersecurity-2026-07-07_en and https://www.euronews.com/my-europe/2026/07/07/brussels-pitches-ai-cybersecurity-plan-amid-dependence-on-us-models
